# PATENT WHITESPACE RECONSTRUCTION
## Patent Opportunity Analysis for A5, A7, and A9

**Objective:** Determine patent whitespace for three architecture candidates  
**Status:** Prior Art Mapping (No Optimization)  
**Date:** May 31, 2026

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Key Finding:** A9 (Modular Fault-Tolerant AFPM) contains the largest patent whitespace, particularly in segmented fault-tolerant windings for aerospace starter-generators. A5 (YASA) is crowded by expired core patents but has whitespace in aerospace adaptations. A7 (High-Speed + Gearbox) has significant whitespace in integrated aerospace systems.

**Most Defensible Invention:** Segmented fault-tolerant winding with isolation architecture for aerospace starter-generator (A9).

**Recommendation:** A9 offers best patent opportunity; A7 offers best technical performance but crowded mechanical space.

### 3. Which Specific Invention Statement Is Most Defensible?

**ANSWER:**
> "A segmented axial flux permanent magnet machine for aerospace starter-generator applications, comprising: a plurality of electrically isolated stator segments; independent cooling circuits for each segment; fault detection circuitry for identifying segment failures; and control logic for continued operation at reduced power following segment fault, wherein the machine maintains DO-160G compliance during faulted operation."

**Reasoning:**
- Combines A9 modular architecture with aerospace-specific requirements
- No prior art for segmented AFPM with aerospace fault isolation
- DO-160G compliance element is novel
- Specific technical implementation defensible
- Addresses real aerospace need (continued operation)

**Alternative (A7):**
> "A high-speed axial flux permanent magnet motor integrated with a planetary gearbox for aircraft starter-generator applications, wherein the motor operates above 6000 RPM and the gearbox provides reduction to engine starter speeds, with integrated lubrication and cooling systems."
