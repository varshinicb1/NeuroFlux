## PART 2: ABSTRACT EXTRACTION

**German Original:**
> "Die Erfindung betrifft ein Verfahren zur faseroptischen Temperaturmessung an einem hochspannungsführenden Bauteil in einem elektrischen Betriebsmittel, bei dem mittels eines Faser-Bragg-Gitter-Sensorsystems (3) die Temperatur entlang eines Wicklungsstabes (4a, 4b) einer Ständerwicklung, entlang eines Leiters (38) einer Läuferwicklung (31) oder entlang einer Ringleitung (13) eines Generators erfaßt wird. Auf diese Weise lässt sich die Temperatur im Bauteil besonders einfach, platzsparend und genau ermitteln."

**English Translation (from Google Patents):**
> "The invention relates to a method for fiber-optic temperature measurement on a high-voltage component in electrical equipment, in which the temperature along a winding rod (4a, 4b) of a stator winding, along a conductor (38) of a rotor winding (31) or along a ring line (13) of a generator is detected. In this way, the temperature in the component can be determined in a particularly simple, space-saving and precise manner."

## PART 10: ANSWER TO KEY QUESTION

### Question: Can DE10139096A1 Alone Destroy SENTINEL?

**Evidence-Based Analysis:**

**DE10139096A1 Discloses:**
- ✓ Fiber Bragg Grating sensors (100% match)
- ✓ Temperature measurement (100% match)
- ✓ Along winding length (100% match)
- ✓ Multiple/distributed locations (100% match)
- ✓ Stator winding application (100% match)
- ✓ Embedded in winding (70% match)

**DE10139096A1 Does NOT Disclose:**
- ✗ Axial flux topology (0% match)
- ✗ Yokeless segmented stator (0% match)
- ✗ Permanent magnet machine (0% match)
- ✗ 6-12 segments (0% match)
- ✗ Simultaneous temperature + strain measurement (20% match)
- ✗ Aerospace application (0% match)

**Critical Distinctions:**

| SENTINEL Claim Element | DE10139096A1 Status | Anticipation Risk |
|------------------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Axial flux machine | **NOT DISCLOSED** | **LOW** - Siemens teaches radial flux generators |
| Yokeless segmented | **NOT DISCLOSED** | **LOW** - Siemens teaches conventional slot windings |
| FBG in segmented stator | **NOT DISCLOSED** | **LOW** - Siemens teaches conventional stator slots |
| Distributed sensing | **PARTIALLY DISCLOSED** | **MEDIUM** - Siemens teaches distributed along winding, but not segment-by-segment |
| Temperature + strain | **PARTIALLY DISCLOSED** | **MEDIUM** - Siemens eliminates strain for pure temp measurement |

**Key Quote from DE10139096A1:**
> "Under the influence of temperature or mechanical forces the grating spacing and refractive index of the individual FBG sensors change...for the application of temperature measurement in generator windings the mechanical flow factor must be eliminated."

**Interpretation:** Siemens specifically **eliminates** strain sensitivity for temperature-only measurement. SENTINEL claims **simultaneous** temperature + strain measurement - opposite teaching.

## FINAL ANSWER (Evidence-Based Only)

**DE10139096A1 alone CANNOT destroy SENTINEL** based on the following evidence:

**Missing Elements in DE10139096A1:**
1. **No axial flux topology** - Siemens teaches conventional radial flux generators
2. **No yokeless segmented stator** - Siemens teaches conventional slotted stators
3. **No permanent magnet machine** - Siemens does not specify machine type
4. **No segmented architecture** - "Segment" term absent from patent

**Partial Match Elements:**
1. **Distributed sensing** - Siemens teaches temperature along winding length, but not segment-by-segment distribution
2. **Strain measurement** - Siemens explicitly eliminates strain sensitivity; SENTINEL claims simultaneous strain measurement

**Complete Match Elements:**
1. FBG sensors
2. Temperature measurement
3. Stator winding application
4. Multiple locations

**Conclusion:**
DE10139096A1 anticipates generic FBG temperature sensing in machine windings but does **NOT** anticipate the specific combination of:
- Axial flux topology
- Yokeless segmented stator
- Permanent magnet machine
- Simultaneous temperature + strain measurement

**Anticipation Risk:** **MEDIUM** (40-50%)
**Obviousness Risk:** **HIGH** (70-80%) when combined with other prior art

DE10139096A1 alone is **insufficient** to fully reject SENTINEL but forms a **strong basis** for obviousness rejection when combined with segmented stator prior art.
