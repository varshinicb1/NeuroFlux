## PART 5: FINAL ANSWER

### Question:
> "If YASA, DSSR, SSDR and FOSM had never existed, what AFPM aerospace starter-generator architectures would we invent from scratch today?"

### Answer:

**PRIMARY INVENTION: Flux-Switching AFPM (C3)**

**Why:**
- **Mechanism:** Flux-switching eliminates rotating magnets (simplification)
- **Physics:** PMs stationary in stator, switched by reluctance rotor
- **Value:** Reduced rotor complexity, higher speed capability
- **Patentability:** Distinct from all existing AFPM topologies
- **Aerospace fit:** Simpler rotor = higher reliability

**Architecture:**
```
- Stator: Segmented with concentrated PMs between teeth
- Rotor: Simple iron with flux barriers (no magnets, no windings)
- Windings: Concentrated around stator teeth
- Core: Laminated with engineered barriers
- Flux: Switched by rotor position, no rotating excitation
```

### Key Insight:

**Without prior architectures constraining thinking, we invent:**

1. **Simplification** (flux-switching, no rotating PMs)
2. **Manufacturing** (PCB stator, no winding labor)
3. **Robustness** (multi-airgap, tolerance insensitive)
4. **Control** (hybrid excitation, field weakening)
5. **Performance** (superconducting, extreme density)

**Mechanism-first synthesis yields different architectures than starting from existing topologies.**

**The "best" architecture depends on which mechanism dominates:**
- Simplicity → Flux-Switching
- Manufacturability → PCB Stator  
- Robustness → Multi-Airgap
- Flexibility → Hybrid Excitation
- Performance → Superconducting
